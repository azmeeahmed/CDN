In These Times, 2020.

HTML, CSS, Javascript, Audio.

The unprecedented global escalation of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 was as abrupt as it was unexpected. Seemingly overnight, millions of lives were turned upside down as nations were advised to stay indoors in an attempt to mitigate the spread of this incredibly infectious - and potentially fatal - disease. 

The continual revision of lockdown deadlines compounded the abiding sense of uncertainty, as people not only worried for the safety of their friends and family, but also wondered when they would be able to see them again. Amidst this anxiety, a new tone began to emerge within our digital interactions, as carefree conversational openings and signoffs became replaced by cautious euphemisms, attempting to acknowledge - in the softest terms possible - these sudden and dramatic shifts in circumstance.

Inevitably, this language quickly spread from the personal to the commercial, becoming co-opted by businesses and corporations seeking to trade off the notion that their presence in our lives provided a reassuring constant in these otherwise uncertain times; discordant piano music and black and white visuals, which inevitably resolved into melody and color at the promise of a commercial product or service, rapidly became a trope of early-COVID TV and pre-roll advertising.

During the summer of 2020, I contributed to the 'Euphemizing The Pandemic' community board (initiated by Clark Dude) on the Are.na website, where users were invited to submit newly encountered phrases that added to the rapidly expanding COVID vernacular. 'In These Times' reads a selection of the euphemisms collected by the community, offset against a soundtrack which compiles the opening musical phrases from various Covid-19 commercials, looped from the youtube video 'Every Covid-19 Commercial is Exactly the Same' by Microsoft Sam.
